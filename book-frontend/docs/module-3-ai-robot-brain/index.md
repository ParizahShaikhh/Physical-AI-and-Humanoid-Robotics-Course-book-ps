# Module 3: The AI-Robot Brain (NVIDIA Isaac™)

## Overview

Welcome to Module 3 of the Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics Course. This module focuses on NVIDIA Isaac technologies for building the AI "brain" of humanoid robots, combining photorealistic simulation, synthetic data, and accelerated perception and navigation. You'll learn how to apply NVIDIA Isaac technologies to create AI-driven humanoid robots.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:
- Understand NVIDIA Isaac Sim and its role in AI training
- Use photorealistic simulation for synthetic data generation
- Explain Isaac ROS and hardware-accelerated perception
- Understand VSLAM and navigation for humanoid robots
- Conceptually integrate AI perception with ROS 2 control

## Prerequisites

- Completion of Modules 1 and 2 (ROS 2 fundamentals and simulation concepts)
- Experience with ROS 2 and simulation environments
- Understanding of basic robotics concepts
- Familiarity with AI and machine learning concepts is helpful

Begin with the first chapter to understand the transition from simulation to intelligence, then progress through each chapter sequentially to build your knowledge of NVIDIA Isaac technologies for AI-driven robotics.
